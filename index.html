Understanding Electricity

What is Electricity?

Electricity is the flow of electrons through a conductor. In EVs, this flow powers the motor that turns the wheels.

Technical Deep Dive:

  • Electron Flow: 6.24 × 10¹⁸ electrons per second = 1 Ampere
  • Conductors: Materials with free electrons (copper, aluminum)
  • Resistance: Opposition to electron flow, measured in Ohms (Ω)
  • Ohm's Law: Voltage = Current × Resistance (V = I × R)

How Electricity is Measured

  • Voltage (V): Electrical pressure - like water pressure
  • Current (A): Flow rate - how many electrons per second
  • Power (W): Voltage × Current - total energy per second
  • Energy (kWh): Power × Time - total energy used

Advanced Measurements:

  • Frequency (Hz): AC cycles per second (60 Hz in US)
  • Power Factor: Efficiency of power delivery (0.95-0.98 for EVs)
  • Harmonics: Distortion in AC waveform
  • Power Quality: Voltage stability and consistency

Battery Technology

EV batteries store electrical energy chemically and convert it back to electricity when needed.

Battery Chemistry:

  • Lithium-Ion: Most common, high energy density
  • Cathode: Lithium cobalt oxide, nickel, manganese
  • Anode: Graphite with lithium ions
  • Electrolyte: Lithium salt in organic solvent

Battery Management System (BMS):

  • Cell Balancing: Equalizes charge across all cells
  • Temperature Control: Maintains optimal 15-35°C range
  • State of Charge (SoC): Monitors battery level
  • State of Health (SoH): Tracks battery degradation

Electric Motor Technology

EV motors convert electrical energy to mechanical energy with high efficiency.

Motor Types:

  • Permanent Magnet: High efficiency, rare earth magnets
  • Induction Motor: No magnets, robust design
  • Switched Reluctance: Simple, low cost

Motor Control:

  • Inverter: Converts DC to AC for motor
  • PWM Control: Pulse-width modulation for speed
  • Regenerative Braking: Converts motion back to electricity
  • Efficiency: 85-95% vs 25-30% for gas engines

Power Loss Factors

  • Aerodynamic Drag: Increases with speed²
  • Rolling Resistance: Tire friction with road
  • Accessories: HVAC, lights, infotainment
  • Battery Heating: Temperature management
